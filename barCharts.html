Dates of Books Writing

This chart shows the number of books written each year from 1500 to 1800. An important conclusion that can be drawn here is the two explosions of growth that occur are around the years 1620 and 1715, the two years that form the , debated, lower bounds of the age on enlightenment

Books Published In London Between 1600 and 1700

This chart shows the number of books published in London between 1600 and 1700. Two interesting conclusions that can be drawn from this chart is two dips in the number of books published a year.

The first could be a coicidence but it's novelty justifies its inclusion as in 1666, the year of the Great Fire of London, there were no books published in London suggesting a major disruption to normal city life caused by aforementioned great fire.

The second is a little more concrete, there is a major dip in the number of books published in the years following 1687. An argument can be made that the Glorious Revolution of 1688 and the subsequent Jacobite rebellions and war with France are the cause of this dip in book publications.
